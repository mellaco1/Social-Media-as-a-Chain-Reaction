Social Media as a Chain Reaction

The world is undeniably addicted to social media, and there's no way to truly stop it.
By showing the world the effects of positive and negative content on average people, we might be able to change things.

The Bottom Line

Here's the thing. Technology is as unstoppable as it is worldwide. But with that right comes some responsibility. Technology must be used the correct and appropriate way. Thinking before you post is one of the clear ways to do this.

It just takes a second... and suddenly 2.3 billion (yes, billion) people have your words in their hands. Before you share your opinion with the world, think to yourself. Is what I'm saying true? Is it hurtful? Because anything and everything that you say can be traced back to you. It could affect a potential career, relationships, and your life as a whole.

Begin

You are free to post about anything, at any time. Don't take this for granted. When you draft up a post, take your time. You can tell the world anything you want to, but you want to be careful.

Rethink

Before you hit the share button, take a moment to talk to yourself. You want to make sure that what you are saying won't damage someone else's career, or even yours. With a slip of the hand, you could be exposing someone for something they don't want publicized. You could turn a friend against you because you said something online that you should've talked about in person.

Get Ready

With your readjusted mindset, you can rewrite your anticipated post. If you want to tell someone something, think about telling them in person, versus through a screen. Your words could come accross as harsh or rude, when you might have meant nothing of the sort. You'll be less stressed if you get a handle on your urge to mind vent on social media. You don't want your friendships to suffer.

Post

When you are ready, and have proofread your desired post (because we all have a tendancy to make typos), go ahead and post! You've edited and now feel comfortable with a general audience viewing what you put out there. You are aware and will now take full responsibility for the response that comes your way. Just think about this: the response will be more positive than if you had put an un-filtered post out into the world.

The Chain Reaction

If you don't think before you post, this can start a Chain Reaction.

chain re·ac·tion

/ˈˌCHān rēˈakSHən/

a series of events, each caused by the previous one.

When someone posts something positive or negative on the Internet, everyone has the instant ability to view and respond to it. It can accidentally influence a particular person... For example, if someone posts a news story about a gruesome death, the people viewing this post can subconciously think about it for the rest of the day. People unfortunately have a tendancy to soak up the negative content more than the positive. It could unintentionally make them sad, or distant.

Controversely, if a post is made about a new baby or a wedding, the reactions will be much different. It is a generally positive post, so it can therefore alter a person's day or mindset in a more positive way!
